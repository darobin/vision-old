This document captures W3C's vision for action in the world.

It was created based on a brainstorming breakout during Team Day 2013. It is an evolving, unofficial, not-particularly-sanctioned, likely-wrong-in-many-places write-up of our collective thinking.

The Web Society

An organisation that does not envision and continuously strive to effect a state of the world sufficiently aligned with its goals that it will then, having attained its endgame, disband and cease its activities of its own accord runs the risk of progressively renouncing its ambitions in order solely to perpetuate its own existence.

Such goals may be lofty. They may be far away and hard to reach. The path leading to them from the present situation may be murky and strenuous, if it can be seen at all, but the destination must be clearly conceived for the organisation to continue applying itself towards their realisation and escape the drift towards a self-preservation of the means over the ends and its attendant bureaucracy.

These goals cannot also be solely technical. Technology devoid of social embodiment is at best a toy; social aspirations without the technical means to deploy themselves are but fantasy. Technology does not create its social counterpart by fiat; and vice versa. A harmonious society demands that humankind's tools and laws must be crafted in unison. It is thus the fundamental objective of the World Wide Web Consortium to better an unfinished revolutionary technology in an incompletely reformed social order until this revolution is completed and in accord with its social correlate.

The Universal Web

access for all, works for all, all can publish

builds a better world

Decentralised Control

democracy, fairness, interoperability, open markets, unified regulation

Better Web, Better World

the two are indistinguishable, privacy & safety everywhere, democracy, rationality, science, inclusive, universal community, a world of abundance

Actions

issue statements based on values, join (lead) internet protests when needed, do policy work, provide input to treaties, e.g. copyright & DRM
